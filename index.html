About Me

My name is Adam Wise and I am a PhD Candidate and NSF Graduate Research Fellow at the University of California, Berkeley. I sit in the Civil and Environmental Engineering department and am advised by Professor Tina Chow. My research focuses on multiscale atmospheric modeling applied to wind energy. In addition to my academics, I am an Engineering Intern at the floating offshore wind start-up Aikido Technologies in San Francisco, CA. Prior to starting my PhD, I was a Fulbright Scholar and Researcher in the Department of Marine Technology at the Norwegian University of Science and Technology advised by Professor Erin Bachynski-PoliÄ‡. There, I studied wake meandering effects on floating offshore wind turbines. In 2018, I completed my undergraduate degree in Mechanical Engineering at Virginia Tech with a minor in Naval Engineering as an Honors Scholar. As an undergrad, I spent the 2015-2016 academic year as an exchange student in Lund University, Sweden.

Research

My research interests... - wind turbine wakes - floating wind - atmospheric flows: stable and unstable - large eddy simulation - boath wind turbine design and atmospheric science
